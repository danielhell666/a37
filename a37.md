# Design System Strategy: The Cinematic Precision Framework

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**The Creative North Star: "The Silent Manifesto"**

This design system is built on the philosophy of discipline, precision, and architectural silence. Inspired by the brand’s concept of "Estética não é vaidade, é estilo de vida" (Aesthetics isn't vanity, it's a lifestyle), the interface must feel like a premium, cinematic editorial. 

We break the standard "template" look by utilizing heavy tonal depth, asymmetrical layouts that prioritize negative space, and a typographic hierarchy that feels authoritative yet understated. The experience should mimic a high-end physical space: solid, layered, and meticulously organized. We avoid the "digital-first" clutter by treating every screen as a composition of light and shadow rather than a grid of boxes.

---

## 2. Colors
Our palette is rooted in the depth of a forest at dusk, contrasted with the warmth of sand and the metallic sharp edge of bronze.

### Tonal Foundation
- **Primary Foundation:** `primary_container` (#122F2D) and `background` (#071616). These deep greens provide the "Cinematic" atmosphere.
- **Warm Contrast:** `tertiary` (#C5C9B1) and `primary` (#AECDC9) act as the light-diffusing elements against the dark core.
- **The Golden Accent:** `secondary` (#FFB77A) and `on_secondary_container` (#EEA565) are reserved strictly for high-conversion CTAs and critical brand moments (mimicking the bronze/gold in the logo).

### The "No-Line" Rule
Traditional 1px borders are strictly prohibited for sectioning. Boundaries must be defined by:
1.  **Background Shifts:** Transitioning from `surface` (#071616) to `surface_container_low` (#0F1E1E).
2.  **Tonal Transitions:** Using subtle gradients to suggest the end of a container without a hard stroke.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as a physical stack. A "Service Card" shouldn't have a border; it should be a `surface_container_high` (#1E2D2D) element sitting on a `surface` background. For high-importance floating menus, use **Glassmorphism**: apply `surface_variant` (#283737) at 60% opacity with a `backdrop-blur` of 20px.

---

## 3. Typography
The system uses **Poppins** as its geometric anchor, supplemented by **Plus Jakarta Sans** for display moments to add a contemporary, architectural edge.

- **Display (Display-LG/MD):** Large, low-tracking (letter-spacing: -0.02em) headers in `on_surface`. Use these for hero statements.
- **Headlines (Headline-LG/MD):** Used for section titles. Ensure generous line-height (1.4+) to maintain an editorial feel.
- **Body (Body-LG/MD):** Rendered in Manrope or Poppins. Use `on_surface_variant` (#C1C8C6) for secondary body text to reduce visual noise.
- **The "Bronze Label":** Use `label-md` in `on_secondary_container` (#EEA565) with 0.1rem letter spacing for category tags or "Premium" indicators.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth
Depth is not achieved through "shadows," but through **Light Absorption.**

- **The Layering Principle:** 
    - Base Level: `surface_dim` (#071616)
    - Card/Section Level: `surface_container` (#132222)
    - Interaction Level: `surface_bright` (#2D3C3C)
- **Ambient Shadows:** Only used for floating action buttons or modal overlays. Use a tinted shadow: `rgba(2, 32, 30, 0.4)` (a deep green tint) with a blur of 40px and 0px spread.
- **The "Ghost Border":** For input fields or cards that require high accessibility, use `outline_variant` (#414847) at 15% opacity. It should be felt, not seen.

---

## 5. Components

### Buttons (The "Precise Geometry" Style)
*   **Primary:** Solid `secondary_container` (#6D3A00) with `on_secondary_fixed` (#2E1500) text. Use `roundedness.md` (0.375rem). The hover state should transition to a subtle bronze gradient.
*   **Secondary:** No fill. `ghost_border` with `on_surface` text. 
*   **Interaction:** On tap/click, use a scale-down transform (0.98) to mimic physical resistance.

### Cards & Booking Lists
*   **Forbid Dividers:** Use `spacing.8` (2.75rem) to separate list items or shift the background color slightly.
*   **Anatomy:** Image-heavy. For service lists (Haircut, Beard), use a `surface_container_low` background with a `tertiary` accent bar on the left (2px width) to denote selection.

### Input Fields
*   **Minimalist Frame:** No top, left, or right borders. Use a single bottom border using `outline_variant` (#414847).
*   **Active State:** The bottom border transforms into a `primary` (#AECDC9) 2px line.

### Appointment Chips
*   **Selection:** Use `primary_container` (#122F2D) for unselected and `secondary` (#FFB77A) for selected states. Precise geometry is key—keep corners at `roundedness.sm`.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
*   **Embrace Asymmetry:** Place a large headline on the left with a primary CTA shifted to the right to create "Visual Tension."
*   **Use Intentional Space:** Use `spacing.20` (7rem) between major sections to let the design breathe.
*   **Focus on Texture:** Use subtle noise overlays on large `background` areas to mimic high-end paper or architectural stone.

### Don't:
*   **Don't use pure black:** Always use `surface` (#071616) to maintain the cinematic forest-green undertone.
*   **Don't use 100% opacity borders:** This breaks the "Silent Manifesto" philosophy and makes the UI feel like a generic app.
*   **Don't clutter the Hero:** Keep the hero section focused on one "Display-LG" headline and one primary "Golden" CTA. 
*   **Don't use standard shadows:** Avoid grey shadows; they muddy the sophisticated palette. Always tint shadows with the primary deep green.

---

## 7. Spacing Utility
All spacing must follow the geometric scale to maintain "Precise Geometry."
- **Internal Padding:** `spacing.4` (1.4rem)
- **Section Gaps:** `spacing.12` (4rem) to `spacing.16` (5.5rem)
- **Component Tightness:** `spacing.1.5` (0.5rem) for label-to-input relationships.```
